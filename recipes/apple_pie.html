Apple Pie

Description

Apple Pie is a pie or a tart, in which the principal filling ingredient is apple. It is, on occasion, served with whipped cream or ice cream on top, or alongside cheddar cheese. The pastry is generally used top-and-bottom, making it a double-crust pie; the upper crust may be a circular or a pastry lattice woven of crosswise strips. Depending on the baker's preference, the bottom of the double-crust may be baked first (before baking the whole pie) to prevent the bottom from getting soggy. Exceptions are deep-dish apple pie, with a top crust only, and open-face Tarte Tatin.

Ingredients

  • Pastry for 9-inch pie
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • 6 cups thinly sliced, peeled apples (6 medium)
  • 1 tablespoon butter or margarine

Directions

  1. Heat oven to 425Â°F. Prepare pastry.
  2. Stir together sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, salt and flour. Mix with apples. Turn into pastry-lined pie plate; dot with butter. Cover with top crust, which has slits cut in it; seal and flute. Cover edge with 2- to 3-inch strip of aluminum foil to prevent excessive browning; remove foil during last 15 minutes of baking.
  3. Bake 40 to 50 minutes or until crust is brown and juice begins to bubble through slits in crust. Serve warm if desired.
